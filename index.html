Tectonic Plate Boundaries

The Earth's outermot layer, the lithosphere, is made of of the crust and solid mantle. The crust varies between oceanic crust and continental crust.

Convergent Boundaries

Definition: Plates push into each other. One may go upward to form mountains, or one may get pushed under the other.

Tectonic Processes: One plate gets pushed under the other (subduction), or they both break and crak.

Geographical Features: trenches, mountains, volcaoes

Plate Tectonic Theory

Definition: The Earth's lithosphere is broken into large and small plates. These plates move on the more flexible asthenosphere through processes including mantle convection.

Divergent Boundaries

Definition: Plates move away from each other.

Tectonic Processes: Magma rises up and forms new crust as plates leave each other.

Geographical Features: Midocean ridges, rift valleys, volcanic activity.

Transform Boundaries

Definition: Plates slide past each other horizontally.

Tectonic Processes: Friction builds up until it suddenly releases causing earthquakes.

Geographical Features: Fault lines and cracks. No volcanoes form at these boundaies because there's no sinking or magma rising

Himalayas – Indian & Eurasian Plates

Boundary: Convergent | Subtype: Continental–Continental

The Indian Plate is crashing into the Eurasian Plate, but becaus both are a similar weight , they dont really move and one doesnt go under the other. instead, the land gets pushed up and forms mountains

Hazards: There are lots of earthquakes and landslides here, but no volcanoes, because no magma is being made under the ground.

Andes – South America

Boundary: Convergent | Subtype: Oceanic–Continental

The Nazca Plate, which is under the ocean, is sliding underneath the South American Plate. As it sinks, it lets out water, which helps melt the rock. This magma rises up and makes volcanoes.

Hazards: Big earthquakes, tsunanis, and volcanos can erupt.

Japan – Western Pacific

Boundary: Convergent | Subtype: Oceanic–Oceanic

The Pacific Plate is sinking under the Okhotsk Plate (which is part of the North American Plate). This creates a chain of volcanic islands, which includes Japan.

Hazards: Earthquakes under the ocean, tsunamis, and volcanos can also erupt

Mariana Trench: Western Pacific

Boundary: Convergent | Subtype: Oceanic Oceanic

The Pacific Plate is sliding underneath the smaller Philippine Sea Plate. This has created the Mariana Trench, the deepest place in the ocean almost 11,000 meters (36,000 feet) deep.

Hazards: underwater earthquakes and tsunamis.

East African Rift

Boundary: Divergent | Subtype: Continental–Continental

The African continent is splitting apart with a giant crack that streches over 3000km. As the land moves away from itself, magma comes up from below and forms volcanoes. In 10 to 50 million years, this area might turn into a new ocean.

Hazards: Earthquakes, volcanic eruptions, and the ground sinking in some places.

Iceland – Mid Atlantic Ridge

Boundary: Divergent | Subtype: Oceanic–Oceanic

Iceland is right on top of the Mid Atlantic Ridge, where the North American and Eurasian plates are slowly moving away from each other. This means the country is being pulled apart. One side is moving toward North America, and the other toward Europe!

Hazards: Volcanic eruptions, hot springs and geysers, and cracks forming in the land.

Mid Atlantic Ridge & East Pacific Rise

Boundary: Divergent | Subtype: Oceanic–Oceanic

These are long mountain ranges at the bottom of the ocean where plates are slowly moving apart. Magma comes up from below and cools to form new ocean floor. At the Mid-Atlantic Ridge, this happens at about 1.5 cm per year on each side.

Hazards: Small underwater earthquakes, hot vents on the ocean floor, and lava flows under the sea.

San Andreas Fault: California

Boundary: Transform

The Pacific Plate and North American Plate slide past each other at the San Andreas Fault. When parts of the plates get stuck and then suddenly move, it causes strong earthquakes.

Hazards: Big earthquakes and cracks in the ground. No volcanoes here because the plates aren’t pushing down or pulling apart.
